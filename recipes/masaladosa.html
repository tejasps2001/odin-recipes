Masala Dosa

Unleash your taste buds with the crispy and flavorful explosion of a traditional Masala Dosa, filled with a mouthwatering potato-based filling!

Masala Dosa is a traditional South Indian dish that is a popular breakfast and street food snack. It is a crispy and savory crepe made from a fermented rice and lentil batter, filled with a spiced potato and onion mixture and served with sambar and coconut chutney.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups idli rice or parboiled rice
  • ½ cup urad dal (husked black gram)
  • 1 tablespoon chana dal (husked and split bengal gram)
  • 20 methi seeds (fenugreek seeds)
  • ⅓ cup thick poha (flattened rice)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil or Butter or Ghee for cooking

Recipe Instructions

  1. Soak the lentils and fenugreek seeds in 1 cup of water for 4 to 5 hours.
  2. Rinse the rice and poha and soak them in 2 cups of water for 4 to 5 hours.
  3. Drain the lentils and grind them with ½ cup of the soaked water or fresh water until light and fluffy.
  4. Drain the rice and poha and grind them with 1 cup of fresh water until smooth.
  5. Mix the two batter and add salt to taste and leave it to ferment overnight.
  6. Heat a griddle and pour a ladleful of batter and spread it to make a thin crepe.
  7. Cook for a minute or two and add oil or butter or ghee.
  8. Flip the dosa and cook for another minute.
  9. Fill the dosa with prepared spiced potato and onion filling and fold it to serve with sambar and coconut chutney.
